Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services, testing procedures, and harm reduction approach

Harm reduction is a set of practical strategies and ideas aimed at reducing negative consequences associated with drug use. It recognizes that drug use is a complex, multi-faceted phenomenon and that abstinence is not always a realistic or desirable goal for everyone. Harm reduction approaches include providing accurate information about substances, testing services, and creating safer environments for use.

How accurate are your testing methods? â–¼

Our testing methods are highly accurate and validated. We use multiple analytical techniques including NMR spectroscopy, LC-MS, and FTIR spectroscopy. Each method has different strengths:

  • NMR provides definitive structural identification
  • LC-MS offers precise quantification down to ppm levels
  • FTIR enables rapid screening and functional group identification

All results include uncertainty measurements and are cross-validated using multiple techniques when necessary.

How much sample do I need to provide? â–¼

For most analyses, we recommend providing approximately 30-50 milligrams of sample. However, the exact amount depends on the type of analysis:

  • Basic identification: 10-20 mg minimum
  • Full quantitative analysis: 30-50 mg recommended
  • Plant/mushroom samples: 50-100 mg (due to lower active compound concentrations)
  • Liquid samples: 0.5-1 mL

If you have limited sample quantity, please contact us to discuss the best analytical approach.

What substances can you test for? â–¼

We can test a wide range of substances including:

  • Psychoactive drugs (MDMA, LSD, psilocybin, etc.)
  • Stimulants (cocaine, amphetamines, cathinones)
  • Opioids (heroin, fentanyl, oxycodone)
  • Dissociatives (ketamine, PCP)
  • Research chemicals and novel psychoactive substances
  • Steroids and hormones
  • Plant materials and herbal preparations
  • Supplements and performance enhancers

If you're unsure whether we can test your specific sample, please contact us with details.

Is the testing process anonymous? â–¼

Yes, absolutely. We prioritize your privacy and anonymity:

  • All samples are assigned unique identification codes
  • Personal information is encrypted and stored separately from sample data
  • We do not share personal information with third parties
  • Results are only accessible with your unique analysis ID and authentication code
  • Sample materials are properly disposed of after analysis

Our privacy policy is GDPR compliant and we follow strict data protection protocols.

Is drug checking legal? â–¼

The legal status of drug checking varies by jurisdiction. In Spain, where we are located, drug checking services operate in a legal gray area but are generally tolerated as harm reduction services. We operate as a research and analytical chemistry service, not as a drug checking service per se.

Important legal considerations:

  • We do not return tested samples to users
  • We operate as an analytical chemistry laboratory providing information services
  • All results are provided for informational purposes only
  • Users are responsible for complying with local laws

We recommend checking local laws in your area before submitting samples.

How long does it take to get results? â–¼

Analysis time depends on the complexity of the sample and the type of testing requested:

  • Basic qualitative analysis: 3-5 business days
  • Full quantitative analysis: 5-7 business days
  • Complex samples with unknown compounds: 7-10 business days
  • Rush analysis (when available): 1-2 business days (additional fee applies)

You'll receive an email notification when your results are ready. Results can be accessed through our secure online portal.

How can organizations access your spectral libraries? â–¼

Our spectral libraries are provided free of charge to verified harm reduction organizations under Creative Commons license. To access:

  1. Contact us at organizations@kykeonanalytics.com
  2. Provide proof of your organization's harm reduction mission
  3. Complete our organization verification process
  4. Receive access credentials and download links

Available formats include Thermofisher/Omnic, Bruker/Opus, and individual .JDX files. We also provide training and support for library implementation.

What should I do if dangerous substances are detected? â–¼

If we detect dangerous substances or high-risk adulterants in your sample:

  • We immediately flag the result as high-risk
  • Detailed safety information is provided with the result
  • We may issue a public alert if the substance poses immediate danger to the community
  • We provide harm reduction advice specific to the detected substances

We strongly recommend not consuming any sample that tests positive for dangerous substances like fentanyl, NBOMe compounds, or other high-risk adulterants.

Can you detect new or unknown substances? â–¼

Yes, our analytical approach is designed to identify both known and novel substances:

  • NMR spectroscopy can determine molecular structure of unknown compounds
  • High-resolution mass spectrometry provides molecular formulas
  • We compare unknown spectra against our growing database
  • Novel substances are flagged for further characterization

If we detect an unknown substance, we'll notify you and may request additional sample material for full characterization.

How do I interpret my results? â–¼

All results include detailed interpretation and safety information:

  • Clear identification of all detected compounds
  • Purity percentages with uncertainty ranges
  • Adulterant identification and concentrations
  • Risk assessment based on detected substances
  • Harm reduction recommendations
  • Reference to published safety data when available

We also provide raw spectral data for independent verification and include safety warnings for any detected dangerous substances.

Do you share results with law enforcement? â–¼

No, we do not share individual results with law enforcement or any third parties. Our commitment to privacy includes:

  • Complete confidentiality of all client information
  • No reporting of individual results to authorities
  • Anonymous market monitoring data only (no personal information)
  • GDPR-compliant data protection protocols
  • Secure data storage with encryption

We only share anonymized, aggregated data for research and public health purposes, never individual results or personal information.
